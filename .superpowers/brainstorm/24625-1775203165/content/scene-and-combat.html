The Arena (Kitchen Floor)

  • 4 boundary walls (countertops / kitchen walls)
  • Fixed top-down camera angle (~60° looking down)
  • Camera follows the player smoothly
  • Enemies spawn from the 4 edges

Inferno

Large fire AOE circle at player position. High damage, large radius.

Auto-target: centered on player

WATER + WATER

Tidal Wave

Expanding water ring that pushes enemies outward. Large knockback.

Auto-target: expands from player

STONE + STONE

Fortress

Stone walls rise around player. Blocks enemy movement for 4 seconds. Player is shielded.

Auto-target: surrounds player

FIRE + WATER

Steam

Cloud of steam that stuns/slows all enemies in radius for 3 seconds.

Auto-target: centered on player

FIRE + STONE

Meteor

A single massive rock falls on the nearest enemy. Highest single-target damage.

Auto-target: nearest enemy

WATER + STONE

Mud

Creates a mud zone on the ground. Enemies inside move at 50% speed for 5 seconds.

Auto-target: zone at player position

Enemy Types & Scaling

Wave 1-3

Slow Slime

Small cube/sphere. Low HP, slow speed. Walks toward player and does contact damage.

Wave 4-6

Fast Slime

Same size but 2x speed. More enemies spawn per wave. Overwhelms with numbers.

Wave 7+

Tanky Slime

Larger model, 3x HP. Slow but hard to kill. Mixed with fast slimes.

The Hungry Golem (Boss — after Wave 7+)

3x size, 10-20x HP, very slow. Commands space rather than chasing. Uses 3 telegraphed attacks:

Heat Wave (Fire)

Glows red 2s → red circle on ground → massive fire blast, pushes player back

Salt Rain (Stone)

3-5 red circles appear near player → salt crystals fall from sky into circles

Deep Soak (Water)

Sprays water stream, rotates slowly → hit = Soaked (50% slow for 3s)

Boss cycles through attacks with ~5s pause between each. Small slimes spawn during the fight to keep pressure up.

Collision Detection

Simple distance-based checks in useFrame() — no physics library needed for MVP:

  • AOE spells: check if distance(enemy, spellCenter) < spellRadius
  • Meteor: check if distance(enemy, impactPoint) < meteorRadius
  • Enemy contact damage: check if distance(player, enemy) < contactRadius
  • Boss telegraphs: same circle-based checks on the red warning zones
